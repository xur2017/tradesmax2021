Analyze Competitive Companies¶

We are going to analyze on how one company performs with regard to its competing companies. Let us assume we are interested in a few big companies in technology sector: Apple, Google, Microsoft, Facebook, Amazon.

Percentage change will find how much the price changes compared to the previous day which defines returns.
Correlation will help us see how one stock's return is affected by the other stocks' returns.

In [4]:
rets = df.pct_change()
corr = rets.corr()

From the Scatter Matrix and Correlation Heatmap, we can find great positive correlations among the competing stocks. However, this might not show causality, and could just show the common trend in the technology companies.

In addition to correlation, we also can analyze each stocks risks and returns. In this case we are extracting the average of returns (Expected Return) and the standard deviation of returns (Risk). Logically, you would like to minimize risk and maximize return.

In [7]:
plt.figure(figsize=(8,8))
plt.scatter(rets.mean(), rets.std())
plt.xlim([0.0, 0.002])
plt.ylim([0.0, 0.02])
plt.xlabel('Expected Return')
plt.ylabel('Risk')

for label, x, y in zip(rets.columns, rets.mean(), rets.std()):
